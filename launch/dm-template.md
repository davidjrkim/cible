# DMs to 10 job-searching contacts

Personal, one-to-one, not copy-paste. The template is a starting point — tailor at minimum the first sentence and the role/company they're targeting.

Goals of these DMs, in order:
1. Get an honest user on the tool today (the warm audience finds bugs the cold one won't report).
2. Get a public reply or repost from someone whose network overlaps mine.
3. Reconnect with people I've been meaning to message anyway. The launch is the excuse.

Do NOT make this a mass DM. If you can't personalize the first line, don't send it.

---

**Template:**

> Hey [name] — saw you were [applying to X / job searching / interviewing at Y, whatever's specific]. I just shipped a tool that tailors CV bullets, a cover letter, and likely interview questions to a specific job posting in ~12 seconds: https://cible.work
>
> Free, no signup, and I built it partly because I'm doing the same job hunt right now. Would genuinely love to know if it gives you something usable for one of your actual applications — or if it falls down, where.
>
> No pressure to share or reply unless it's useful. If it does work for you, a quick post is the kind of thing that actually helps me land a job, so I'll take it.

---

**Variants by context:**

- *Close friend who'd help anyway:* skip the framing, just send the link and ask them to break it.
- *Someone applying to a specific company:* offer to run their CV against that JD personally and send them the result, rather than asking them to do the work.
- *Someone who's a stronger LinkedIn voice than I am:* be explicit that a repost is the ask. They'll respect the directness.

**Don't send to:** recruiters at companies you're actively interviewing with — the launch coverage is fine for them to see organically, but a DM asking them to try your portfolio tool is awkward.
