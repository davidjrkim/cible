# Applied AI Engineer — Harvey

**Company:** Harvey
**Location:** San Francisco or London
**Team:** Applied AI

## About the role

Harvey builds generative AI tools for elite law firms. Our customers include Allen & Overy, PwC, and dozens more. We sit between frontier model providers (OpenAI, Anthropic) and lawyers who have zero tolerance for hallucination.

The Applied AI team is responsible for the prompts, pipelines, evals, and retrieval systems that turn frontier models into reliable legal products. This is not research; it is the unglamorous engineering that makes "AI for lawyers" actually work at the standard our customers expect.

## What you'll do

- Build and maintain LLM pipelines for legal-document tasks (contract drafting, due diligence, redlining)
- Run rigorous evals on every prompt change. We do not ship a prompt without a passing regression check.
- Improve retrieval quality on dense legal corpora (BM25 + dense, hybrid rerankers, chunking strategies)
- Reduce hallucination through grounding mechanisms — verifiers, citations, source attribution
- Work with our Product team to translate fuzzy legal requirements into measurable eval criteria

## What we look for

- Strong software engineering background (Python or TypeScript), 4+ years
- Hands-on experience with LLM APIs (OpenAI, Anthropic) in production
- You have shipped, evaluated, and iterated on at least one real LLM product
- You think in terms of evals, not vibes. You can explain a confusion matrix.
- Bonus: experience with legal, medical, or financial domain — anywhere hallucination has real cost

## What we don't expect

- A research background. Most of our best engineers have never written a paper.
- Familiarity with law.

## Compensation

Competitive base + equity. SF or London.
